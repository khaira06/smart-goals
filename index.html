IT Help Desk SMART Goals (2025)

By: Surinder Singh
Student Number: 991788351

Mission

To provide responsive, reliable, and customer-focused IT support that enhances user productivity and satisfaction across all departments.

Vision

To build a proactive, knowledge-driven help desk that sets the standard for service excellence and continuous improvement in the coming years.

SMART Goals

Goal 1 – Improve Customer Satisfaction

SMART Goal: From January 1, 2025, to December 31, 2025, we will improve our HDI SCS Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) score by 5 percentage points (from 86% to 91%) by focusing on better communication, agent coaching, and consistent follow-up with end users.

Measurement: Progress will be tracked monthly through HDI SCS survey results and quarterly reports.

Relevant: Customer satisfaction is one of the most important HDI SCS indicators of service quality and directly reflects how end users view the help desk.

Reference: MetricNet. (2023). Service Desk Benchmarking and Metrics. Retrieved from https://www.metricnet.com

Goal 2 – Solve More Issues on the First Call

SMART Goal: From February 1, 2025, to December 31, 2025, we will increase our HDI SCS First Contact Resolution (FCR) rate by 7 percentage points (from 65% to 72%) by strengthening our knowledge base, empowering agents to make decisions, and providing cross-training for common issues.

Measurement: FCR will be measured weekly using ticketing system analytics (number of resolved incidents without escalation ÷ total incidents).

Relevant: A higher FCR rate reduces repeat tickets, improves efficiency, and provides users with faster, more reliable support.

Reference: ServiceNow. (2024). Top 10 IT Service Desk Metrics That Matter. Retrieved from https://www.servicenow.com

Goal 3 – Resolve Tickets Faster

SMART Goal: From March 1, 2025, to December 31, 2025, we will reduce our HDI SCS Average Resolution Time (MTTR) by 10% compared to last year’s baseline by improving ticket triage, making better use of automation, and addressing repeat issues at the root cause.

Measurement: Resolution time will be monitored daily through the ITSM tool and reviewed in monthly trend reports.

Relevant: Reducing average resolution time means users get back to work faster, improving productivity and aligning with HDI best practices.

Reference: Atlassian. (2024). Measuring IT Service Desk Performance: Key KPIs. Retrieved from https://www.atlassian.com

Goal 4 – Meet SLA Commitments More Consistently

SMART Goal: From April 1, 2025, to December 31, 2025, we will increase our HDI SCS SLA Compliance rate by 8 percentage points (from 88% to 96%) by using proactive monitoring, quicker escalations, and coaching agents to stay within SLA timelines.

Measurement: SLA compliance will be measured monthly using SLA reports from the ITSM system.

Relevant: Meeting SLA targets builds trust with stakeholders, shows reliability, and demonstrates accountability.

Reference: Freshworks. (2023). Help Desk Metrics: 10 KPIs to Track for Better Support. Retrieved from https://freshworks.com

Goal 5 – Reduce Abandoned Calls and Wait Time

SMART Goal: From May 1, 2025, to December 31, 2025, we will decrease our HDI SCS Call Abandonment Rate by 20% (from 5% to 4%) by refining staffing schedules, offering call-back options, and streamlining queue management.

Measurement: Call abandonment and average wait times will be tracked weekly via the call management system.

Relevant: Lowering abandonment and wait times ensures customers feel valued, reduces frustration, and strengthens the overall support experience.

Reference: ITSM.tools. (2023). 8 Tips for Better IT Service Desk Metrics. Retrieved from https://itsm.tools
